Q. 45

A boat can travel 20 km downstream in 24 min. The ratio of the speed of the boat in still water to the speed of stream is 4:1. How much time will the boat take to cover 15 km upstream?

A 20 mins
B 25 mins
C 30 mins
D 40 mins

Detailed Solution

Correct Answer: Option C (30 mins)

Let speed of boat in still water = \( 4x \) km/hr
Let speed of stream = \( 1x \) km/hr

Downstream speed = \( (4x + x) = 5x \) km/hr

Given, distance = 20 km, time = 24 mins = 24/60 hrs = 2/5 hrs.

Speed = Distance / Time
\( 5x = 20 / (2/5) \)
\( 5x = 50 \Rightarrow x = 10 \)

So, speed of boat = 40 km/hr, speed of stream = 10 km/hr.

Upstream speed = \( (4x - x) = 3x = 30 \) km/hr.

Time to cover 15 km upstream = 15 / 30 hrs = 1/2 hr = 30 mins.
